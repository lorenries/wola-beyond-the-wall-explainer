President Trump's Anti-Immigrant Agenda

An Explainer of Where Things Stand in Congress

By Adam Isacson, produced by Loren Riesenfeld• September 3, 2017

With Congress back in session, there are several bills concerning U.S.-Mexico border security and immigration that could soon come to a vote. These bills include an approximate $1.6 billion request for President Trump’s border wall, as well as requests to expand the law enforcement agencies that identify and deport undocumented immigrants. The Trump administration’s decision to phase out Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) will also likely influence how Congress handles these bills, which will be debated in the coming months as Congress works to approve the are supposed to be rejected or approved by October 1 along with the rest of the 2018 fiscal year budget.

Below are profiles of the various border security and immigration bills under consideration in Congress, including our assessment of what will likely happen next, our view of the proposed legislation, and our recommendations for what you can do to take action against these costly, divisive and ineffective proposals.
